Digital Footprint

The trail of your actions that you leave behind online.

What is your Digital Footprint?

A digital footprint is the trail of data you leave behind when using the internet, including social media posts, website visits, emails and online purchases.

  • It can be active like your social media posts, comments, emails and filling out online forms.
  • It can be passive like your IP addresses, browsing history, cookies and more.

Why does it matter?

Your footprint impacts your reputation as employers, schools or acquaintances may view this informaiton. Moreover, a large digital footprint can make you a target for phsihing or scams.

How to Manage Your Footprint:

📸

Being cautious of what you post

Be cautious of the pictures you post online because those pictures can be re-shared by friend, family and colleagues without your consent.

Remember: once on the Internet, always on the Internet.

🔐

Restrict mobile app permissions.

Be selective of the information you grant access to a mobile app, includign your photos, location, camera, contacts and more.

🗑️

Limiting online accounts/Deleting old accounts.

You can reduce your footprint by deleting or deactiving accounts that you no longer need. Moreover, be wary when linking accounts to websites that you do not use frequently because it may be more effective to check out as a guest rather than creating an account.

💡 Quick Tip

Think before you click, post, or share--everything you do online leaves a footprint that can last forever.
