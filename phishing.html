Phishing

When someone pretends to be a store or person you trust to trick you into giving your password, money, or info.

What Is Phishing?

Hackers send fake emails or messages that look real. They want you to click a link or type your password so they can steal it. Don't fall for it!

How to Spot It

  • Weird or fake sender address
  • Scary or rushy words like "Act now!" or "Your account will close!"
  • "Dear Customer" instead of your name
  • Buttons that say "verify" or "confirm": don't click. Go to the real website yourself.

      💡 Quick Tip

      When you're not sure, don't click the link. Open the real website yourself (type the address or use your bookmark) and log in there.

      Scams Over Calls

      Scammers don't only use email. They also call or leave voicemails pretending to be your bank, tech support, or the government. They want you to give passwords, pay "fees," or let them into your computer.

      • Don't trust caller ID. Scammers can fake the number so it looks like a real company.
      • Never give passwords or codes over the phone. Real banks and companies won't ask for these on a call.
      • If someone pressures you to act "right now," hang up. Call the real company yourself using the number on their official website or your card.
