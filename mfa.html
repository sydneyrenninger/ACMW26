Multi-Factor Authentication

Extra lock on your account — so even if someone knows your password, they still can’t get in.

What Is MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)?

You prove it’s really you with more than one kind of check.

  • Something you know (like a password)
  • Something you have (like your phone)
  • Something you are (like your fingerprint)

Why Use It?

Passwords can get stolen. With MFA, the bad guy still can’t get in without your phone or fingerprint.

Three Ways to Prove It’s You

🤖

Something you know

Password or PIN

📱

Something you have

Your phone, a code, or a key

😁

Something you are

Fingerprint or face

💡 Quick Tip

Use an app like Google Authenticator or a security key when you can. Turn on MFA for email and important accounts first.
